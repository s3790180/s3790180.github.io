Project Ideas.

Overview

Augmented reality (AR) is a technology that uses a mobile device’s camera to view the surrounding environment with additional information, usually in the form of computer generated visual information.

My project idea is a mobile app and digital platform with features targeted at tourists and locals who like to explore their surroundings. Using augmented reality, it combines a digital travel guide, audio tour and travelogue together, and enhances the users experience when traveling and visiting historical locations. This includes mapping of historically interesting locales, providing narrative in audio and overlaying objects on top of locations on screen, such as interactive buttons to access relevant information or historic photo comparisons and before and after images.

Introducing gaming elements on top of this allows users to also participate in events such as scavenger hunts or great race styled activities.

Motivation

Tourism is 1 of 5 super growth industries in Australia, with 9.1 million international visitors per year1. That means there are over 9 million potentially users with room for growth locally. And these are just national numbers. Internationally, tourism contributed over 7.6 trillion dollars in 20162 and augmented reality is expected to have 1 billion users by 20203. According to an ISACA survey, 60% to 70% of consumers see clear benefits in using AR and IoT devices in their daily life3. As these statistics show, these markets have a lot of untapped potential to invest in.

Description

The app would be a combination of the following:

Digital travel book app: this would include mapping functions that would allow users to see sites of interest on their mobile device’s screen and plan out their trip. It could provide an itinerary for popular tours and adjust on the fly depending on user traffic so that popular tourist attractions would not be inundated with travellers at the same time and allow for a more enjoyable experience. This can also involve business establishments to provide services as well.

Audio tour app: A popular tool for places such as museums, we can provide pieces of information relevant to the user and their current location, and not force the user to follow a set menu.

Travelogue: This is the more personal side. With users creating accounts, they can document they journey, save photos taken (including augmented ones), as well as share with family and friends.

Augmented reality app: The augmented reality function is the part that ties the all the above together, as well as introduce additional layers to it all. What it would do is, when users are at locations of interest, they will have the ability to interact in various ways. Icons on their screen to access relevant information (whether it be visually as text or aurally as voice over). Logical next locations could be suggested. Overlaying historical images on their screen as they use their camera, so the user can compare what’s currently on their screen to historical images. For example, seeing how a building might have looked 20, 30 or maybe 100 years ago, or even what was there before it was a building. These could be provided as historical photos, artist renditions or computer generated imagery. As the technology gets more advanced, we can introduce animation and video, such as re-enactment of key moments in history to be displayed on their screen, overlayed on the images that their cameras are seeing, in real time.

Gaming and gamification:
Scavenger hunts and Great Race styled activities: With the database of information on tourist spots and local sites, we can leverage all this data to create hints and tips for users to find interactive objects on their screen around their current location. So instead of using physical opjects such as QR codes to provide next steps, we use AR objects to replace them to tick off their digital score book, or provide the next step of their journeys. Both these activities would be linked through the network to provide up-to-date information between all competitors.

Backend: Just as the mobile device is important for the user as the point of interaction, the back-end will be just as important to collate all the data to provide to the users. How we will create this database will be two-fold. First by contracting the relevant organisations (such as Tourism Australia) and secondly, by crowdsourcing users to provide their own content for access on the platform.

With this app, we can give to the user an experience that they can have at their own pace, or information that they themselves want more off. With augmented reality becoming more obtainable on mobile devices, it’s becoming a feature with more value as it gets more robust and developed.

Tools and Technologies

We’ll need to utilise the augmented reality software libraries of the two largest mobile platforms (Android and iOS), and front-end development for these platforms. Also important is development for a web-based portal, to allow users another avenue of interaction, as well as allow access for organisations and users who will deal with the large amounts of data and information that would be accessed.

On the backend side, there will be need to develop a platform (servers) that allows users to upload images, sound and other media and content. Other technologies that would be required include access to mapping APIs, as the data from such platforms as google maps is already quite mature and data rich, which alleviates the need to build our own.

Other technologies that will be required include: server platform (such as AWS); mapping services (such as Google Maps API); and a security platform for all the data that this app will need access to.

Skills Required

My lack of knowledge about detailed skills in this space makes it hard to be more specific, but speaking in general terms, we’ll need a team a developers to work on the various parts of the app.

Front-end developers to work on at least 3 different platforms, Android, iOS and web. We may need a team dedicated to working on the AR side of things.

Then we will need a team of software engineers to develop the back-end solutions for this app. This will probably be the larger team, as they would need to work on things such as real-time networking solutions as well cross-platform compatibility.

Outcome

This project would launch in in waves, starting with one or two specific locations (cities), preferably with high tourist concentrations, and as user uptake climbs, open up the platform for more locations nationally. When the app gains more traction, we can open up the platform to an international level. Although the app is aimed at the tourism industry, we can open up the platforrm to other organisations, such as schools, where they can use this app as an educational tool, allowing for students to learn more about their surroundings and its histories.
